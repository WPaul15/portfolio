FAQ

Willem? Like Willem Dafoe?

Yes. Although his given name is actually "William". Mine is "Willem". You can pronounce it "WILL-um" (how English-speakers—myself included—say it) or "VILL-ehm" (how it should actually be pronounced). It's the Dutch version of the name "William", akin to the German "Wilhelm".

Why did you choose software engineering?

To me, computer science is too theoretical and computer engineering is too "physical", so to speak. Ever since my sophomore year of high school I knew I wanted to study something software-related. After learning about software engineering, I knew that it was what I wanted to study. I think software engineering is the perfect balance of technical and soft skills. I enjoy coding, no doubt, but I also enjoy creating and designing software on a higher level. I like the idea of working with clients to gather system requirements because I don't want to end up in a position where the products I create don't have immediate impact on peoples' lives. I want to use software to make a difference in the world and I feel I can do that best through software engineering.

What are you going to do with German?

I began learning German in high school. When I got to ISU, I knew I wanted to continue learning German and taking courses, so I did. A classmate in my first German course told me about the Languages and Cultures for Professions (LCP) Program and how it would be a perfect fit for me. The LCP Program offers a specialized second major or minor to students in the Colleges of Engineering, Business, or Agriculture and Life Sciences that better prepares them for work in our globalized workforce. It really was the perfect fit for me: It aligns well with my personal goals, allows my German course credits to count towards something, allowed me to study abroad in Salzburg, Austria for a year, and will continue to open many doors for me in the future. I hope to eventually find work in a German-speaking country.

Learning another language has a lot of benefits besides just knowing another set of vocabulary and different grammar. It has also helped me be more confident in my speaking, both in English and in other languages. I've also increased my cultural awareness and global competency, i.e. I now have a better sense of how to interact with people from other cultures and I appreciate our differences more.

The short answer: Anything I want.
